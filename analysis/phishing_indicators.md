# Phishing Indicators Analysis

## Common Phishing Indicators

### 1. Suspicious Sender Domain
Attackers often use fake domains similar to legitimate companies.

Example:
- paypa1.com
- amaz0n-support.com

---

### 2. Urgent or Threatening Language
Phishing emails create panic to force users into immediate action.

Examples:
- "Your account will be suspended"
- "Immediate verification required"

---

### 3. Generic Greetings
Legitimate organizations usually use customer names.

Suspicious examples:
- Dear User
- Dear Customer

---

### 4. Suspicious URLs
Malicious links often:
- contain unusual words
- use random domains
- imitate trusted brands

Example:
http://secure-login-verification.com

---

### 5. Dangerous Attachments
Common malicious file types:
- .exe
- .zip
- .scr
- .docm

---

## Risk Indicators Summary

| Indicator | Risk Level |
|-----------|------------|
| Fake domain | High |
| Urgent language | Medium |
| Generic greeting | Medium |
| Suspicious attachment | High |
| Credential request | Critical |

---

## Conclusion
Phishing emails rely heavily on social engineering techniques rather than technical exploitation. Users should always verify sender identities and avoid clicking suspicious links.
